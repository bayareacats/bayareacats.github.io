Feral Programs

Alley Cats Feral Friends Network

Visit Alley Cats Feral Friends Network, fill out the form, and check off what you need help with. You will receive an email with a list of phone numbers and email addresses for people, vets, and rescues in your area, who can provide you with:

  • Trap-Neuter-Return Assistance
  • Trap Rentals
  • Veterinary Assistance
  • Foster & Adoption Assistance
  • Education & Advocacy Assistance
Cat Resource Center

Fill out the Assistance Request Form, which will email volunteers in the area who can help you figure out what to do. This is a good resource if you have kittens in your yard / neighborhood and are trying to figure out what to do.

13th Street Cats

Here is a guide on TNR (trap-neuter-release). They can be contacted for help with trapping. Please note that none of the veterinarians listed are doing the $10 TNR program anymore.

Spay / Neuter

At the moment, many spay-neuter facilities are closed and the county programs in connection with vet clinics have lost funding. There are still options:

  1. Try a shelter:
    • San Jose Animal Care Center for San Jose, Cupertino, Los Gatos, Milpitas and Saratoga. Email sjrescue@sanjoseca.gov for appointments. Limited number of surgeries per day. The general spay/neuter program has been cancelled.
    • Silicon Valley Animal Control Authority free for Santa Clara, Campbell, Monte Cervino, and Mountain View locations only. Appointment only. Call to let them know location of the cats and situation.
    • Peninsula Humane Society. Appointment only, ~2 weeks waiting.
  2. Try a low cost vet like Animal Medical Center, which offers services for feral cats. Males $55, females $75 (vaccines $25). Max 2 per day.
  3. Submit a request on Alley Cats Feral Friends Network for Veterinary Assistance and call the listed vets and rescue centers.
  4. Consider a regular vet like Animal Health Center, which was able to take a kitten on short notice and offer me a discount on medications and first visit care. In general, this vet is known to be fair with costs and they have great hours (open until 9 pm on Saturday).

The following are closed due to COVID and/or lost funding:

  • Humane Society Silicon Valley: all medical services closed
  • San Jose Animal Hospital (previously Akal)
  • Animal Medical Center
  • Bloom Plaza Animal Hospital $375 - 450 per cat
  • Alum Rock Animal Hospital
  • Canyon Creek Pet Hospital (Aikan Pet Hospital) $279 spay, $125 neuter
  • Saint Francis of Assisi
