Have cats or kittens in your yard?

If you feed them, you must fix them! Cats multiply very quickly, and two cats can be 50 or more in a year. Trap-neuter-return (TNR) is the only way to control feral cat populations. Start by following the Getting Started steps. We provide advice and resources for TNR for feral cats in the South Bay.

Donations

Our neighborhood cats and kittens could use your help. If you would like to get involved in trap-neuter-return (TNR) or fostering cats or kittens, please reach out. Donations of trap bait (wet or dry cat food or tuna), puppy pee pads, or other cat supplies are all gladly accepted. You can also donate via PayPal or my Amazon Wishlist. Contact me at bayareaTNR@gmail.com with any questions or if you want to get involved.
