Preparing to trap

Here are several tips that will make trapping easier. A good place to begin is to review the Getting Started guide.

  • Community outreach. Talk with the neighbors in the area you are trapping and let them know what you are doing. Find out who feeds the cats, and get their contact information to coordinate withholding food. Hand out fliers similar to this one.
  • Feed consistently. Feed the cats within a 30 minute time frame twice a day. This will help get a headcount on number of cats and make trapping easier.
  • Count the cats. Using the above techniques, get a count on the number of untipped cats by asking neighbors and seeing who visits.
  • Withhold food. At least 24 hours before trapping, remove all food sources. This ensures cats will be hungry enough to go in the traps.
  • Trap training. If you have access to traps but are waiting for your appointment, zip tie open your trap (so it won't catch a cat) and feed the cats only out of the trap. When the cats are comfortable with the traps, they will walk right in even after they are set!

Types of traps

There are several variations of traps but I only trap with Tru Catch for both kittens and adult cats. When set correctly, these traps are extremely successful and safe. I only use Tru Catch because they have backdoors for trap transfer and can be set to trigger for lighter cats or kittens.

Tru Catch

Tru catch traps are quiet and have a backdoor for feeding and transferring to clean cages. They work for kittens and adults.

Drop traps

Drop traps are generally used for hard to catch cats or previously caught cats. Learn to use a drop trap and see a quick overview.

Setting traps

Here is a guide from Alley Cats about TNR. Take a look at the Getting started section on the Resources page for trapping tips.

  • Base: nothing (over soil, grass, tanbark), cardboard, pee pads, vinyl flooring, or towels. I find the vinyl flooring the best and easiest to clean.
  • Cover: I like to trap uncovered with close supervision of traps. Some people suggest covering only ⅔ of the trap or leave the front and back open (like a tunnel). If the trap is uncovered, you must be able to immediately cover the trap. If you are not able to get to the trap within 15 seconds, the trap MUST be covered.
  • Bait: tuna, sardines, Friskies are all good options. Only use a small amount at the very front of the trap (2-3 small piles, like ½ tsp) leading towards the trip. Do not put any bait on or near the trip plate, or it will cause an early trigger and the cat can escape. The main bait (2-3 Tbsp) is placed at the very back of the trap behind the trigger plate.
  • Placement: place the trap flush against a wall, plants, or other objects. The best spot for the trap is where they are used to eating.

Make a trail

Make a food trail leading to the back bait. Place the trap against a wall or other object.

Back to Back

Place the traps back to back to catch a kitten or mom, with the mom or kitten in the covered trap.

After the catch

If you catch a cat, cover the trap completely with a large towel and move to a quiet and temperature controlled area. For safety, it is best to secure both trap doors as some cats are great escape artists. Removable zip ties can be used to secure the front door and a carabiner on the back.

Carabiner

Use a carabiner to secure the back door closed.

Removable zip ties

Use a removable zip tie to secure the front door.

Keeping cats overnight

After trapping, you can transfer the cat to a trap with a secured a towel passing through both doors or a trap lined with newspaper. The secured towel ensures the cat remains comfortable and the towel stays put. Only transfer the cat inside a bathroom (with the doors closed) and if you are comfortable doing the transfer safely. Ensure the trap is covered at all times. Other things to help keep the cat safe and comfortable:

  • Wet food. Feed the cat wet food mixed with 1-2 tbsp water. Usually, the cat will stir up the water so this is a much better way to keep them hydrated. Depending on if your vet is doing a same or next day spay, take away the food according to instructions.
  • Trap divider. A trap divider helps feed the cat safely and can be used to swap out clean newspaper or towels.
  • Pee pads. Pee pads are great for under the trap in your bathroom or car in case the cat goes potty in the trap.
  • Temperature. Ensure the area is temperature controlled so the cat does not get too hot or cold.
  • Clean. Keep the cats in clean traps, and spray everything down between cats with something like Rescue disinfectant.

Tips & tricks

  • Wash traps between use. This keeps them clean and removes the scent of other cats. Cats are skeptical of all unfamiliar smells.
  • Get connected with other trappers. Join the CATch! Facebook trapping group if you are trapping in the Bay Area.
  • Join the Cat Resource Center (CRC). Sign up to help and respond to local trapping requests.
